Name: Apple
Look for: Well shaped, smooth skinned fruit that is free of bruises. Brownish freckled areas do not affect flavour
Store: Store in perforated plastic bags in the refrigerator crisper. Apples soften 10 times faster at room temperature
Prepare: Rinse and eat. To prevent browning, coat cut surfaces with lemon juice
Nutrition: Vitamin C, high in fibre, sodium-free

Name: Banana
Look for: Firm, unblemished bananas. Yellow with brown speckles indicates a sweet tender fruit
Store: Keep at room temperature until desire ripeness. For longer storage, once ripe, refrigerate. The peel will darken but remains fresh
Prepare: Peel and eat. To prevent browning, coat surface with lemon juice. Use over ripe bananas in baking
Nutrition: Vitamin C, high in potassium, source of fibre, fat-free, sodium-free

Name: Avocado
Look for: Ripe, soft fruit. Avoid fruit with dark sunken spots or bruises.
Store: Cover and refrigerate ripe or cut fruit for up to five days. Ripen firm fruit at room temperature until it yields to gentle pressure
Prepare: Rinse, remove pit by cutting lengthwise around seed. Gently twist to separate halves. Peel skin. To prevent browning, coat with lemon juice
Nutrition: Vitamin K, B5, B6, E, A, C, high in fibre, potassium, folate, etc.

Name: Tangerine
Look for: WFirm, heavy fruit with glossy orange skin
Store: Refrigerate covered for several weeks
Prepare: Peel and eat fresh. Use sections to garnish desserts or poultry based dishes
Nutrition: Vitamin C, fat-free, sodium-free

Name: Coconut
Look for: Shake to check if there's water in the coconut. If there's no sound, the coconut is too ripe and soapy
Store: Store a whole coconut in the fridge for up to two months. Opened coconut meat can be kept covered in the fridge for up to 7 days
Prepare: Eat coconut meat plain, or grate it for use in a variety of dishes
Nutrition: Vitamin C, high in manganese, iron, zinc, phosphorus, selenium, copper

Name: Strawberry
Look for: Firm, plump berries that are fully red with bright green caps. Strawberries do not ripen once picked
Store: Arrange unwashed on paper towel in shallow pan. Loosely cover, refrigerate unwashed for up to two days or freeze for up to one year
Prepare: Gently rinse, pat dry, then remove caps
Nutrition: Vitamin C, fat-free, sodium-free

Name: Grape
Look for: Plump grapes that are firmly attached to green stems. Avoid wrinkled or sticky fruit
Store: Cover, refrigerate unwashed for up to five days
Prepare: Rinse just before serving
Nutrition: Fat-free, low in sodium

Name: Guava
Look for: Firm, green or yellow, free of blemishes
Store: Cover and refrigerate ripe fruit for a day or two. Ripen at room temperature, out of direct sun until yields to gentle pressure
Prepare: Cut in half crosswise and scoop out flesh or peel and thinly slice. Use in desserts and fruit salad
Nutrition: Source of potassium, high in folacin, vitamin C, sodium-free

Name: Mango
Look for: Plump fruit without green background colour that yields to gentle pressure. Avoid soft, brusied or shrivelled
Store: Ripen fruit uncovered at room temperature. Cover, refrigerate ripe fruit for up to three days
Prepare: Rinse. Score skin in four sections and peel like a banana or cut in half and remove pit. Fruit clings to pit so slice as much as possible from it
Nutrition: Vitamin C, fat-free, sodium-free

Name: Pear
Look for: Firm, well shaped, fruit. Use fully ripe fruit for immediate use. Minor scars and blemishes do not affect flavour
Store: Cover, refrigerate unwashed ripe fruit for up to three days. Ripen firm fruit at room temperature in a paper bag until it yields to gentle pressure at stem
Prepare: Rinse. To prevent browning, coat cut surfaces with lemon juice
Nutrition: Vitamin C, high in fibre, sodium-free, fat-free

Name: Papaya
Look for: Fruit with smooth unblemished skin. Ripe fruit has more yellow than green colour & yields to gentle pressure
Store: Ripen firm fruit at room temperature in a loosely closed paper bag. Cover, refrigerate ripe fruit for up to one week
Prepare: Rinse, cut in half lengthwise. Scoop out seeds. Scope out flesh or peel skin and cut as desired
Nutrition: Vitamin C, K, folacin, potassium, source of fibre, fat-free

Name: Pomegranate
Look for: Large pomegranates, heavy for size
Store: Keep at room temperature for a few days or refrigerate for 1 to 2 weeks
Prepare: Slit skin and carefully pull away skin and pith. Only seeds are edible. Pomegranates are juicy with a sweet tart flavour. Sprinle over salads, desserts or crush seeds in blender and strain to make juice for drinks, sauces and jellies
Nutrition: Vitamin C, K, high in fibre, sodium-free

Name: Cherry
Look for: Plump, bright, coloured fruit. The deeper the colour the sweeter the fruit. Avoid very soft or shrivelled cherries
Store: Arrange unwashed on paper towel in shallow pan. Cover and refrigerate for up to three days
Prepare: Rinse. Remove stems and pits. Eat raw
Nutrition: High in potassium, vitamin C, fat-free, sodium-free

Name: Jackfruit
Look for: Fruits that range in colour from green to yellow. It should have yellow flesh and a noticeable fragrance
Store: Keep it at room temperature for up to 5 days or in the refrigerator for up to 6 weeks
Prepare: Jackfruit is consumed fresh. Discard the core, seeds and skin since they are inedible
Nutrition: Source of potassium, copper, manganese, magnesium, fat-free, sodium-free

Name: Watermelon
Look for: A firm symmetrical melon with fully rounded sides and a yellowish underside. If cut, select a melon with bright red flesh
Store: Whole melons will keep for 7 to 10 days at room temperature. Store them too long, and they’ll lose flavour and texture. After cutting, store wrapped watermelon in refrigerator for 3-4 days
Prepare: Rinse, cut melon into wedges or scoop out flesh
Nutrition: Vitamin C, fat & sodium-free
